Makroona bel sansa

Description

Tunisian Pasta with sauce known as Makroona bel sansa is a family favorite. The classic pasta shape is one locallay called "fell", short, smooth tubes similar to Italian mezzi tubetti lisci. However, the pasta may take another shape, as shown in the photo above, called "ressor", which the french word for spring.

If you've ever been in Tunisia, you'll know 2 things about Tunisians. They love their food spicy hot and they love their pasta! In fact, after Italians they're the second consumer of pasta in the world, consuming a whopping 12 kg per person a year!

Ingredients

  • 500g -1lb pasta of your choice
  • 350g - 12oz beef, cubed
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 TBS tomato paste
  • Harissa (to taste)
  • 3 bay leaves
  • handful of precooked chickpeas (optional)
  • 4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 hot green chilli (optional)
  • 2 tsp sweet paprika
  • 1 tsp ground cumi
  • 1/2tsp ground craway
  • 1/4 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 tsp dried mint
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • salt, black pepper, olive oil

Steps

  1. In a frying pan or large saucepan, heat the oil. Add the spices and bay leaf. Brown for a few minutes while stirring.
  2. Then the meat to brown. Once browned add the onions and garlic. always over medium heat, stirring.
  3. Lower the heat and add the tomato concentrate, and half liter/half qt of water.
  4. Cover and cook for about 1 hour or until the meat is tender.
  5. Once the meat is cooked, add in the precooked chickpeas (if using) and the pasta. You should have enough water to cover the pasta. Cook the pasta in the sauce for about 10 minutes for al-dente or 12 minutes for well-done.
  6. Spoon into bowls and garnish with black olives, if you like.
